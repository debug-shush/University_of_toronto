UNIVERSITY OF TORONTO

The University of Toronto (U of T), Canada is a global leader in higher education and research. Established in 1827 with its main campus at St. George, Downtown Toronto, Ontario - the university currently imparts world-class education at three different locations in Canada. With more than 980 programs at the undergraduate and graduate level, U of T offers a plethora of courses in an extensive range of disciplines. Whether you want to pursue engineering, arts, medicine, or social sciences, the University of Toronto, Canada has it all. Through its groundbreaking research in stem cell, the discovery of insulin and electron microscope, University of Toronto has not only placed itself among the world’s most prestigious research-intensive universities but is also currently ranked #28 for Impact Rankings 2020 by Times Higher Education. This Canadian university has also been ranked among the global top 30 higher study institutes for decades by prominent ranking agencies such as Times Higher Education (THE), QS Rankings, Shanghai Ranking Consultancy, etc. This multi-campus university offers education to over 93,000 students with over 23,000 international students. Around 80% of student population studies at the undergraduate level and around 95% of the University of Toronto’s graduate students study at the downtown St.George campus. According to US Media Company and Shanghai Ranking Consultancy, few of the top-ranked courses of the university are Clinical Medicine, Psychology, Arts & Humanities, and Finance, a degree in which can help you land some of the high-paying jobs in Canada. U of T also ranks #16 for its graduate employability as per QS rankings (2020), suggesting that students completing their PG degrees from U of T find it easy to get jobs.
